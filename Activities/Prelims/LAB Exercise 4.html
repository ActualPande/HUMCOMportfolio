Why Horror Fans Should Play Indie Studio Project Moon's Games

A Deep Dive into Dark Storytelling and Challenging Gameplay

By: Hans Jayden A. Lopez
February 3, 2025

For fans of the horror genre, discovering games that delve deep into psychological terror and explore dark, unsettling themes is always a thrill. Indie studio Project Moon is a developer that has mastered the art of creating nightmarish, thought-provoking worlds that are far from conventional. Their games, Lobotomy Corporation and Library of Ruina, offer horror fans a unique blend of storytelling, gameplay, and atmosphere. Project Moon's approach to horror is unlike anything seen in mainstream titles, making their games essential experiences for anyone who craves something more intense and cerebral.

What sets Project Moon apart is its ability to combine dark narratives with innovative gameplay mechanics. In Lobotomy Corporation, players are tasked with managing a corporation that contains dangerous entities known as " Abnormalities." While it may sound like a typical management sim at first glance, the game is packed with horrific imagery, moral dilemmas, and existential questions that make players feel the weight of every decision. The horror isn't just in the gruesome visuals, but in the overwhelming sense of responsibility and the ethical compromises the player must face. This type of emotional and psychological horror is a rare but powerful experience.

In Library of Ruina, the terror is no longer just external but internal, as players enter a strange library where the battles they face are rooted in the inner workings of human nature and despair. The game’s turn-based combat is coupled with a narrative that explores the darker side of humanity, including suffering, loss, and the pursuit of meaning. The visceral nature of the combat, combined with the thematic elements of the story, pulls players into a world that’s as haunting as it is profound. For horror fans, Library of Ruina offers a different type of terror, one that stems from understanding the human psyche's fragility and the darkness that dwells within.

Another reason horror fans should turn to Project Moon’s titles is the company's mastery in world-building. Both Lobotomy Corporation and Library of Ruina feature intricate universes full of lore and hidden stories waiting to be uncovered. Every Abnormality, every character, and every piece of dialogue holds a deeper meaning. The sense of mystery is palpable, as the more players explore these worlds, the more unsettling truths they uncover. This level of depth is rarely seen in traditional horror games, which tend to prioritize immediate scares over a complex and evolving world.

Finally, the unique art direction in Project Moon’s games adds another layer of horror that complements the gameplay and narrative. With its distinctive, often grotesque character designs, Project Moon’s visual style pulls players into its dark and twisted worlds. The game’s anime-inspired art, combined with unsettling themes, creates a sense of dissonance—beauty mixed with horror—that amplifies the eerie atmosphere. This juxtaposition between the gorgeous artwork and the disturbing content heightens the emotional impact, offering horror fans a visually captivating yet nightmarish experience.
